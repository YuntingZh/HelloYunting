Ongoing Master’s Thesis

Painting has always been an integral part of my life, a medium through which I express my deepest passion for visual communication. From a young age, drawing helped me connect with and understand the world around me. I remember sketching churches, noting how Catholic crucifixes include Jesus while Protestant crosses stand bare. I also recall studying the light at dusk, noticing how only the tallest points of the highest buildings catch the fading sun. These experiences sharpened my sensitivity to visual details and storytelling through art.
In 2022, when diffusion model AIs transformed the art and design world, I witnessed firsthand how they revolutionized workflows for animators, painters, and designers. The ongoing discussion around AI's influence on the future of creativity led me to question its potential impact on traditional painting. With my background in computer science and interactive media, I naturally became interested in how AI could enhance human-computer interaction (HCI) within art tools.
After exploring the field of human-robot co-creation, I became fascinated by how early models like SketchRNN and pix2pix offer exciting opportunities to blend AI with traditional art forms, particularly in doodle art for early education. In my current master's thesis, I’m investigating how AI can serve as a facilitative tool to enhance and support conventional painting techniques. I would love to hear your thoughts if this intersection of AI and traditional art resonates with you:)
