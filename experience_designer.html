Experience Designer

NASA SUITS Creative Design Challenge

As the Experience Designer, I crafted an immersive AR HoloLens interface for NASA astronauts, focusing on making critical information easily accessible while ensuring the interface remained non-intrusive. My role involved designing a seamless user experience that guided astronauts through mission-critical tasks, using clear visual hierarchies and intuitive interaction models.
Working closely with engineers and UX researchers, I iterated on design prototypes using Figma to refine the AR interface and ensure alignment with astronaut needs. The project involved integrating a language model-based voice control feature to enable hands-free operation, enhancing both usability and safety. Additionally, I utilized animation techniques to provide real-time visual feedback, creating a responsive and engaging user experience.
A significant part of this project involved solving complex design challenges, such as adapting the UI for space environments where conventional interaction models are less effective. Leveraging AI, I collaborated with the backend team to integrate a rover tracking feature that allowed astronauts to interact seamlessly with Leo rovers, further enhancing the depth and interactivity of the experience.

Key Technologies: AR, HoloLens, Voice Control, Real-Time Rendering

2D Animation and 3D Motion Tracking for ShiftPixy

Besides game development, I was also involved in creating a series of motion-based animations that brought the ShiftPixy brand to life by seamlessly integrating animated characters into real-world environments. My focus was on enhancing brand storytelling through engaging and interactive visual content that captivated the audience. I collaborated closely with the creative lab and shooting team, using Blender to animate character movements and employing 3D motion tracking to align digital assets accurately within the physical setting. Additionally, I utilized After Effects for post-production to remove the trackers, ensuring a polished final look. This combination of 2D animation, 3D motion tracking, and refined visual adjustments helped create an immersive and cohesive brand experience, bridging digital storytelling with physical spaces in a unique way.

Mr. Fossil

"Mr. Fossil" is an MR-based educational experience designed to teach children about paleontology in an engaging and interactive way. Utilizing MR technology, users can place and explore 3D fossil models in their real-world environment, learn fun facts, and even interact with animated dinosaurs. The app encourages a hands-on approach to learning, making the experience both educational and entertaining. This project showcases my skills in creating interactive MR content with a focus on education and user engagement.

Key Technologies: Unity, 3D Modeling, Animation

SunWATER Plus

NSF-Funded Project

During my role as a Designer and Researcher on the NSF-funded SunWATER Plus project at UC Berkeley, I collaborated with cross-functional teams, including data analysts and a fabrication team, to design the UIUX of a greywater recycling system for hotels and domestic residentials. My responsibilities spanned from ideation to the production of the software, where I created wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity mockups using Figma, ensuring a sleek and visually appealing design. I also designed an AR feature for solar panel tracking and water filtration animations, enhancing the app’s interactivity. Due to the sensitive nature of the project, specific details are under NDA. Additionally, I conducted user research and gathered feedback to iterate quickly, improving the app’s usability and functionality.
